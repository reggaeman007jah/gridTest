    • Mission Roles

    [MC] Mission Command

    This is a challenging role. This person needs to always have a good grasp of mission progress, through taking to the various element leads. In terms of comms, the MC will have a direct line to a Platoon Commander (via Primary ShWv), and to the Airbourn Lead (via Primary LoWv). So, Mission Command will always be close enough (ideally) to use ShWv (short wave) radio with the PC (Platoon Commander). The MC will also keep in contact with the AL (Airbourne Lead) using LoWv (long wave).

    [PC] Platoon Command

    This role manages the direct instruction and tactical command of all FTL elements, as well as his/her own squad. This role is a combination of both combat and micro-instruction (e.g. check out the green building on the left side of that junction, or, “keep pushing north until you reach enemy contact”). The level of communication between the PC and FTL is likely to be frequent and detailed. This may lead to a secondary channel being implemented. They ensure that the higher level directives are being executed on the ground. They also ensure that any air elements are well informed.

    [FTL] Fire Team Lead

    Fire Team Leads are at the forefront of the action. They are required to direct their teams directly into the fight. Use of C2 here is highly recommended as a command tool. An FTL will normally have a localised objective set by the Platoon Lead. This could be “push down the street and hold the next junction”, or, move east through the neighbourhood to support FT2” etc. The PC essentially sets the tactical direction, with the FTLs as the fighting force. As said above, the PC will also have units assigned to him/her, possibly specialist (AA, Medical, Engineering etc), but equally could be a standard fire team.

    [AL] Airborne Lead

    This person is responsible for troop transport (both human and AI). They are also responsible for creation and deployment of any new resupply units. They also bring in any required ammo supplies. The AL will have both ShWv and LoWv enabled. Primarily their contact will be directly with the MC. However ShWv can be used to communicate with the Platoon Commander, or FTLs (Fire Team Leads) where appropriate. For example, when requesting specific validation / suitability of an LZ, to request new smoke etc, it might be preferable for the pilot and TFL to speak directly.

    Radio Communications

    We use TFAR for in-game communications, routed via a TS service kindly provided by Woofer. As a general rule, shortwave radios are used by combat troops, longwave radios by transport and resupply elements, with command party to most converstations st all times.

    You are expected to have tested your radio equipment before missions, as it can be problematc fixing comms issues during a mission. If you need any assistance setting these up, make sure you reach out to someone beforehand so we can get everything set up and working nice.

    Requesting Assets During Operations

    Casualties and troop resupplies are inevitable. If you are an FTL and require new units for your squad, you should relay this to your PC, who will in turn relay this to MC, who will in turn relay this to the Airbourne Lead. You are encouraged to only request what you need (realism and frames). When you are relaying your requirements up the chain, it is also important that you relay exactly where these units are to be deployed. This can be at a pre-arranged location (probably safe), or a dynamic LZ within the AO if deployment/resupply is urgent. It is good practice to inform the AC of any likely threats in the area, ideally marking directions with green/IR smoke for the LZ and red smoke to indicate the direction of the threat. Requesting deployment near to enemy troops is high-risk, and while these requests will always aim to be provisioned, pilots are able to abort if they feel that conditions are unsuitable. In an abortive drop, the Airbourne element would advise and request a secondary LZ. Lastly, pilots and ground forces alike are expected to know all pre-designated LZs ahead of mission-start, so any deployment to these will not normally need visual marking.
